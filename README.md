What I'm Building
I design the whole pipeline, not just the pretty part — parsing, retrieval, decision logic, citation-checking, and audit trails.

⚖️ Littman — Founder 2025 – present
AI for legal work that turns a firm's playbooks and past documents into cited answers. Every answer can be approved, flagged, or escalated to a human when confidence is low. I built the full pipeline: parsing → chunking → retrieval → fact extraction → a decision engine for the firm's rules → citation verification → an audit log of everything that happened. Next.js TypeScript FastAPI Postgres/pgvector LLM APIs

🍷 OENRA — Creator 2026
Reads a wine's lab chemistry (GC-MS / LC-MS panels) and a person's palate on the same eight dimensions, then predicts whether they'll actually enjoy a bottle — and explains why, instead of handing back a number. Shipped with a short paper on the model and where it breaks down. Next.js React TypeScript Tailwind custom Canvas/SVG

✈️ FlightReady — Creator 2026
A pre-flight go/no-go risk tool for small aircraft. Scores risk four different ways — including a tail-risk model borrowed from finance — and shows exactly how each score was computed. The thesis: a go/no-go call is a bet on the worst likely day, not the average one. Next.js TypeScript Monte-Carlo simulation

🔩 Systems & Reverse Engineering — where I started 2018 – 2021
Taught myself C and C++ by taking games apart and building tools that worked around kernel-level anti-cheat (BattlEye, EAC, Vanguard) — the hardest technical work I'd done, and I was still a kid. Walked away on my own once I realized I'd rather build things for people than against them. That instinct for pulling hard systems apart is still how I approach AI.
